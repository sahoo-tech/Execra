## 🌟 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of **Execra** pledge to make participation in our community a **harassment-free experience for everyone**, regardless of:

- Age, body size, visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression
- Level of experience, education, socio-economic status
- Nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, or color
- Religion, or sexual identity and orientation

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an **open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community**.

---

## ✅ Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- 🤝 **Being welcoming and inclusive** — using language that makes all contributors feel valued
- 🌱 **Supporting newcomers** — everyone starts somewhere; help others grow
- 🧠 **Demonstrating empathy** — being thoughtful and kind in all interactions
- 🗣️ **Giving constructive feedback** — critique the work, never the person
- 📚 **Being open to feedback** — gracefully accepting suggestions on your own contributions
- 🙏 **Acknowledging mistakes** — accepting responsibility and learning from them
- 🎯 **Focusing on what's best for the community** — not just for yourself

### Examples of **unacceptable** behavior:

- 🚫 Sexualized language, imagery, or advances of any kind
- 🚫 Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- 🚫 Public or private harassment of any contributor or maintainer
- 🚫 Publishing others' private information without explicit permission (doxxing)
- 🚫 Dismissing or discouraging contributors based on experience level
- 🚫 Spamming issues/PRs with low-effort contributions just to earn points
- 🚫 Any other conduct that a reasonable person would find unprofessional

---

## 🛡️ Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for **clarifying and enforcing** these standards. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, and other contributions
- Ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behavior violating this Code of Conduct

---

## 🌐 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in **all community spaces**, including:

- The GitHub repository (Issues, PRs, Discussions, Wiki)
- Code review comments
- Official communication channels (email, chat)
- Public events where an individual represents the project

---

## 📬 Reporting a Violation

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at:

**📧 Email:** `youremail@example.com`
**Subject Line:** `[CODE OF CONDUCT] Report`

All complaints will be:
- Reviewed and investigated **promptly and fairly**
- Kept **confidential** — reporters will not be identified without consent
- Responded to within **48 hours**

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

### 1. 🟡 Correction
**Community Impact:** Minor inappropriate behavior (a single rude comment, accidental misstep).
**Consequence:** A private written warning with clarity on the violation and why it was inappropriate.

### 2. 🟠 Warning
**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.
**Consequence:** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. A period of no interaction with the people involved.

### 3. 🔴 Temporary Ban
**Community Impact:** Serious violation — sustained inappropriate behavior or harassment.
**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication within the community for a specified period.

### 4. ⛔ Permanent Ban
**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression toward individuals or groups.
**Consequence:** Permanent ban from all community interaction.
